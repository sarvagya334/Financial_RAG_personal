| Category                     | Policy / Provision                                                                                                  | Tax Treatment & Conditions                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Employer’s Contribution      | 12% Contribution Limit                                                                                              | Contributions up to 12% of basic salary and dearness allowance (DA) are tax-exempt. Contributions exceeding this limit are taxable.                                                            |
|                              | Aggregate Limit                                                                                                     | If the employer’s total contribution to PF and pension schemes exceeds ₹7.5 lakh in a financial year, the excess is treated as a perquisite and taxed according to the employee's income slab. |
|                              | Employer Benefit                                                                                                    | Employers can claim the 12% contribution as a deduction from their own income for tax calculation.                                                                                             |
| Employee’s Contribution      | Section 80C Deduction                                                                                               | Contributions up to ₹1.5 lakh can be claimed as a deduction under Section 80C in the Old Tax Regime. No such deduction is available in the New Tax Regime.                                     |
|                              | Interest on Contributions                                                                                           | Interest is tax-free if the rate does not exceed 9.5%. However, interest earned on employee contributions exceeding ₹2.5 lakh per year (post-March 31, 2021) is taxable.                       |
| Withdrawals                  | Partial Withdrawals (Advances)                                                                                      | Partial withdrawals from the EPF account are entirely tax-free.                                                                                                                                |
|                              | Final Withdrawal (>5 Years)                                                                                         | Final withdrawals are tax-free after 5 years of continuous membership.                                                                                                                         |
|                              | Final Withdrawal (<5 Years)                                                                                         | Generally taxable, unless the withdrawal is due to project completion, establishment closure, or ill health. Transferring the account to a new job also avoids tax.                            |
| Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) | Threshold and Rates                                                                                                 | No TDS is deducted if the withdrawal amount is less than ₹50,000. If over ₹50,000 and voluntary before 5 years, TDS is 10% (with PAN) or 34.608% (without PAN).                                |
|                              | Form 15G / 15H                                                                                                      | Members whose total income is below the exemption limit can submit Form 15G (under 60 years) or Form 15H (60+ years) to prevent TDS deduction                                                  |
